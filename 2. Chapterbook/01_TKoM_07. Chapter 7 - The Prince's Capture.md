# Chapter 7: The Prince's Capture

The heroes crept carefully through the thick brush of the Great Forest. The trees grew older and the shadows grew deeper until suddenly, the woods opened up into a wide, circular clearing. 

This was the ancient Druid's Grove, just like the goblins had said. Giant, moss-covered stone pillars stood in a wide circle, looking like sleepy stone giants watching over the forest. The air felt heavy and tingling with ancient magic.

"Get down," whispered Szeth, dropping low behind a massive fallen log. 

The rest of the heroes quickly ducked behind the log, peering over the rough bark to spy on the center of the clearing. They held their breath, trying to remain as stealthy as possible, though Szeth almost gave them away by accidentally crunching a dry autumn leaf under his boot. *Crunch.*

Aknemeia winced, but luckily, the people in the clearing were too busy to notice. 

Standing right in the center of the stone circle was Ulfrik, the fierce pirate captain who had attacked their ship. He looked strong and intense, holding a beautiful, gleaming silver sword in his hands—the stolen Beastslayer. 

Beside him stood an older, serious-looking man wearing long, flowing robes. He held a wooden staff that hummed with magical energy.

And sitting on a flat stone right between them was none other than Prince Gregory.

He wasn't tied up, and he didn't look scared at all. Instead, he looked incredibly annoyed. 

"This rock is cold," Prince Gregory complained loudly, crossing his arms and pouting. He was acting like a spoiled brat, completely oblivious to the serious danger he was in. "And my boots are muddy! I demand a proper velvet chair and a plate of warm cheese tarts!"

Ulfrik rolled his eyes and ignored the boy, turning to the man in the robes. "Are you sure this is going to work, Audrin? Is the boy's royal blood strong enough?"

Audrin nodded slowly, his face grim. He began drawing strange symbols in the dirt around Gregory with his staff. "Yes, Ulfrik. Queen Alicia used her royal bloodline to bind Kazgoroth into the Shards long ago. Because Gregory is her son, we can use him as a magical conduit to draw the beast's spirit out of the Shards and pull it back into the physical world."

"And then," Ulfrik said, running a thumb along the flat edge of the Beastslayer sword, "I will slay it."

"When Queen Alicia, Suhlur, Keane, and I first trapped Kazgoroth, we did not have this sword to defeat him," Audrin said, pointing his staff at the glowing blade. "Hopefully, we can finally defeat the evil beast once and for all."

Prince Gregory stopped complaining about cheese tarts and looked up, his eyes wide. "Wait. You're trying to summon a giant, evil monster right here? Next to me? Isn't that dangerous? What if we fail?"

Ulfrik looked down at the young prince. His eyes were fierce but sad. "The risk is necessary to bring justice for my people," the Northman said firmly. "Kazgoroth's lies tore my people apart. I will not let it hide in a crystal forever."

Behind the fallen log, the heroes listened in shock. 

Aknemeia grabbed Caryndal's sleeve. "This is terrible!" she whispered. "Summoning a demigod? Even a weakened one? The magic will tear this entire forest apart!"

"She's right," Caryndal muttered, his mask reflecting the dim light of the grove. "And if they fail to defeat it, Kazgoroth will be free to destroy the whole island. We can't let them finish this ritual."

"We need a plan," Eryneth said, squinting through the moss. Her keen elven eyes scanned the clearing. "There are too many open spaces. But look over there—the tall grass near that pillar. I can sneak around and get close to the prince."

"Good idea," Caryndal said, reaching for his lute. "I will hum a distraction tune. Something loud and confusing to draw their attention."

Aknemeia stood up slightly, golden sparks dancing across her knuckles. "And I'll prepare my magic if they try to attack."

Szeth nodded, drawing his weapons with a soft *shring* sound. He bent his knees, ready to sprint into the clearing. "And I will charge in and grab the boy while they are distracted."

The team was ready. They traded determined nods, their friendship and teamwork stronger than ever. It was time to stop a summoning, save an annoyed prince, and hopefully, not get squashed by a giant monster in the process.
