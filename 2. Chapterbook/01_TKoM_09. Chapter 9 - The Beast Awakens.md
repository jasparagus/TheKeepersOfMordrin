# Chapter 9: The Beast Awakens

The giant reptile unleashed a deafening roar that slammed into the heroes like a physical wall of force. High above the Druid's Grove, ancient branches snapped like twigs, and flocks of birds scattered for miles in every direction. The beast was fully formed now, a colossal, terrifying monster of muscle, sharp scales, and teeth the size of swords. 

Szeth, caught by the sheer force of the monster's roar, threw his body over Prince Gregory, shielding the boy from the blowing debris and dark magic whipping through the air.

Below them, Ulfrik and Audrin staggered back, their weapons practically useless against the towering creature. 

"Our magic isn't strong enough!" Audrin yelled over the howling wind, his face pale as he looked up at the demigod. 

"We need the sword!" Ulfrik shouted back, but before he could raise the silver Beastslayer, Kazgoroth swung its massive tail. The blow sent Ulfrik flying backward, his sword clattering to the muddy ground. 

"Did you guys really not think this through?!" Szeth yelled from the edge of the grove, brushing a clump of mud from his white clothes. 

"Let's discuss their poor planning *after* we defeat the giant monster, okay?!" Eryneth shot back, already pulling an arrow from her quiver.

Caryndal scrambled up onto a nearby boulder, his lute in hand. "She's right!" he yelled down at the two men. "We'll help you stop this thing, but we're taking the boy with us!"

Ulfrik groaned, pulling himself up from the mud. He looked at the massive, terrifying reptile, then back at the heroes. He gave a single, begrudging nod. 

The heroes didn't wait for another invitation. They sprang into action, moving not just as four individuals, but as a coordinated team. 

"Hey, ugly!" Aknemeia shouted, launching herself forward. She threw her hands toward the sky, and a massive, brilliant firework display erupted directly in front of Kazgoroth's face. Bright sparks and dragon-shaped flames exploded with a series of loud cracks, blinding the beast and causing it to thrash its enormous head in confusion.

At the same time, Caryndal strummed fiercely on his lute. Using his illusion magic, he projected the sounds of a dozen roaring monsters from the opposite side of the clearing. Kazgoroth spun around, its tiny eyes searching for the phantom threats, its senses completely scrambled.

But the beast was relentless. It blindly snapped its massive, razor-sharp jaws, nearly catching Audrin. 

In a flash of white movement, Szeth dashed into the fray. He leaped into the air, delivering a powerful flying kick directly to the side of Kazgoroth's snout. The blow wouldn't have hurt the demigod normally, but it was just enough to knock its dangerous jaws off course, saving the elven wizard. 

"Now, Eryneth!" Szeth yelled as he rolled away from another wild bite.

Eryneth drew her bow back tightly. Instead of arrows, she fired a volley of glowing, magical nets that she had prepared earlier. The nets unfurled in the air and wrapped tightly around Kazgoroth's thick legs. The beast roared in frustration, trying to tear the magic away, but its own massive weight worked against it. It stumbled, fighting to keep its balance on the uneven, debris-covered ground.

*Thud.* 

The enormous creature fell to one knee, the ground shaking beneath them. 

"Ulfrik! The sword!" Audrin cried out. The elf raised his hands, channeling the very last of his magical energy into the fallen Beastslayer sword. The silver blade began to glow with a brilliant, blinding light. 

But Ulfrik was still too far away, struggling to cross the muddy distance. Kazgoroth was already beginning to tear through Eryneth's nets. 

"I've got you!" Aknemeia yelled. She pointed her hands at the ground just behind Ulfrik and unleashed a controlled, explosive burst of magical force. The shockwave safely propelled the large Northman forward, launching him directly toward the glowing weapon.

Ulfrik hit the ground rolling. He reached out, his hand closing around the hilt of the radiant Beastslayer. With a final, echoing roar of effort, the pirate leader drove the magical blade straight into Kazgoroth's chest.

There was no blood. Instead, the sword acted as a brilliant, blinding focal point. A blast of pure, white light erupted from the blade, matching the intense purple-black energy pouring from the beast. 

Kazgoroth let out one final, world-shaking shriek. Then, in an instant, the colossal monster shattered into a million pieces of swirling, dark smoke and purple-black light. The smoky remnants were forcefully sucked backward, pulled quickly into the shattered fragments of the dark crystal still lying in the dirt. 

And then, it was over.

The howling wind immediately ceased. The dark, unnatural clouds that had choked the sky rapidly parted, letting warm, bright sunlight stream down into the deeply scarred Druid's Grove for the first time in centuries. 

A quiet, heavy silence fell over the clearing. The only sound was the heavy, exhausted panting of the heroes and the two summoners, all of them covered head to toe in mud, soot, and sweat.

Outside the edge of the grove, Prince Gregory slowly peeked out from behind a large rock where he had taken cover. He was wide-eyed and trembling, completely stripped of his usual pompous attitude. 

"Did... did we win?" the young prince asked quietly.

In the center of the grove, Ulfrik leaned heavily on the dulling Beastslayer sword. The large, imposing pirate looked at Aknemeia, Caryndal, Eryneth, and Szeth. After a long moment, Ulfrik lowered his head in a slow, respectful nod of acknowledgment. 

The Keepers looked at one another, tired but smiling. They had pulled off the impossible. They had saved the prince, and together, they had saved the world.
