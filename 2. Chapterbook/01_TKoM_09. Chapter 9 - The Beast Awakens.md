# Chapter 9: The Beast Awakens

The giant reptile unleashed a deafening roar that slammed into the heroes like a physical wall of force. High above the Druid's Grove, ancient branches snapped like twigs, and flocks of birds scattered for miles in every direction. The beast was fully formed now, a colossal, terrifying monster of muscle, sharp scales, and teeth the size of swords. 

Szeth, caught by the sheer force of the monster's roar, threw his body over Prince Gregory, shielding the boy from the blowing debris and dark magic whipping through the air.

Below them, Ulfrik and Audrin staggered back, their weapons practically useless against the towering creature. 

"Our magic isn't strong enough!" Audrin yelled over the howling wind, his face pale as he looked up at the demigod. 

"We still have the sword!" Ulfrik shouted back, but before he could raise the Beastslayer, Kazgoroth swung its massive tail. The blow sent Ulfrik flying backward, the silver sword clattering to the muddy ground. 

"Did you guys really not think this through?!" Szeth yelled from the edge of the grove, brushing a clump of mud from his white clothes. 

"Let's discuss their poor planning *after* we defeat the giant monster, okay?!" Eryneth shot back, pulling another an arrow from her quiver and tying a rope to it.

Caryndal scrambled up onto a nearby boulder, his lute in hand. "She's right!" he yelled down at the two men. "We'll help you stop this thing, but we're having a serios chat when we're done here!"

Ulfrik groaned, pulling himself up from the mud. He looked at the massive, terrifying reptile, then back at the heroes. He gave a single, begrudging nod. "Fine by me!"

The heroes didn't wait another moment. 

"Hey, ugly!" Aknemeia shouted, launching herself forward. She reached her hand out toward the creature, and a massive eruption of sparks erupted directly in front of Kazgoroth's face in the shape of a golden dragon. The bright light and deafening sound caused the beast to thrash its enormous head in confusion, snapping its jaws harmlessly at the air.

At the same time, Caryndal strummed fiercely on his lute. The sounds of a dozen roaring monsters echoed from the opposite side of the clearing. Kazgoroth spun around, its tiny eyes searching for the phantom threats, its senses completely scrambled.

But the beast was relentless. Even confused, it managed to catch Audrin with its massive tail, knocking the elf backwards.

In a flash of white movement, Szeth dashed into the fray. He leaped into the air, delivering a powerful flying kick directly to the side of Kazgoroth's snout. The confused beast focused on Szeth allowing Audrin to gather himself and prepare another spell.

"Now, Eryneth, before I become lunch!" Szeth yelled as he rolled away from another wild bite, beginning to pant.

Eryneth drew her bow back tightly. She fired a volley of arrows, each tied together, in a circle around the beast's legs, tripping it up in a tangle of rope. The beast roared in frustration, trying to free itself, but its own massive weight worked against it. It stumbled, fighting to keep its balance on the uneven, debris-covered ground.

*Thud.* 

The enormous creature fell to one knee, the ground shaking beneath them. 

"Ulfrik! The sword!" Audrin cried out. The elf raised his hands, channeling the very last of his magical energy into the fallen Beastslayer sword. The silver blade began to glow with a brilliant, blinding light. 

But Ulfrik was still too far away, struggling to cross the muddy distance. Kazgoroth was already beginning to tear through Eryneth's nets. 

"I've got you!" Aknemeia yelled. She pointed her hands at the ground just behind Ulfrik and unleashed a controlled, explosive burst of magical force. The shockwave safely propelled the large Northman forward, launching him directly toward the glowing weapon. His eyes widened in surprise, but he gathered himself in midair.

Ulfrik hit the ground rolling. He reached out, his hand closing around the hilt of the radiant Beastslayer. With a final, echoing roar of effort, the Northman drove the magical blade straight into Kazgoroth's chest.

A blast of pure, white light erupted where the blade touched Kazgoroth, matching the intense purple-black energy pouring from the beast. 

Kazgoroth let out one final, world-shaking shriek. Then, in an instant, the colossal monster burst into swirling, dark smoke and purple-black light, and in a final burst, faded into nothingness. 

Just like that, it was over.

The howling wind immediately ceased. The dark, unnatural clouds that had choked the sky rapidly parted, letting warm, bright sunlight stream down into the deeply scarred Druid's Grove for the first time in centuries. 

A quiet, heavy silence fell over the clearing. The only sound was the heavy, exhausted panting of the heroes and the two summoners, all of them covered head to toe in mud, soot, and sweat.

Outside the edge of the grove, Prince Gregory slowly peeked out from behind a large rock where he had taken cover. He was wide-eyed and trembling, completely stripped of his usual pompous attitude. 

"Did... did we win?" the young prince asked quietly.

In the center of the grove, Ulfrik leaned heavily on the dulling Beastslayer sword. The large, imposing pirate looked at Aknemeia, Caryndal, Eryneth, and Szeth. After a long moment, Ulfrik lowered his head in a slow, respectful nod of acknowledgment. 

The Keepers looked at one another, tired but smiling. They had pulled off the impossible. They had saved the prince, and together, they had saved the world.
