# Chapter 9: The Beast Awakens

Kazgoroth unleashed a deafening roar that hit the heroes like a wall. Around the Druid's Grove, tree branches snapped like twigs, and flocks of birds scattered for miles in every direction. The beast was fully formed now, a colossal reptilian monster with armor-like scales and teeth the size of swords. He gave off an evil, purple-black light.

In the center of the grove, Ulfrik and Audrin fell to their knees from the sheer force of the roar. 

Audrin fired a blast of magical energy at the beast, which bounced harmlessly off its scales. "My magic isn't strong enough!" Audrin yelled over the howling wind. 

"I still have the sword!" Ulfrik shouted back. Before he could raise the Beastslayer, Kazgoroth swung its massive tail. The blow sent Ulfrik flying backward, the silver sword clattering to the muddy ground. Unfortunately, it landed right underneath the beast.

"Did you guys think this through *at all*?!" Szeth yelled from the edge of the grove, brushing a clump of mud from his white clothes.

"We can discuss their poor planning *after* we defeat this monster!" Eryneth shot back. She pulled an arrow from her quiver and tied a rope to it.

Caryndal scrambled up onto a nearby boulder, his lute in hand. "She's right!" he yelled down at the two men. "We'll help you stop this thing, but we're having a *serious* chat when afterwards!"

Ulfrik groaned, pulling himself up from the mud. He looked at the massive reptile, then back at the heroes. He gave a single nod, and yelled, "Fine by me! Audrin needs time for a spell, and I need that sword!"

The heroes didn't hesitate.

"Hey, ugly!" Aknemeia shouted, launching herself forward. She thrust her hand toward the creature, and a burst of flames erupted in front of Kazgoroth. The fire took the shape of a dragon as it launched at the beast. The beast thrashed its enormous head in confusion, snapping its jaws at the fire dragon.

At the same time, Caryndal strummed fiercely on his lute. The sounds of an imaginary army echoed around the grove, created from Caryndal's magic. Kazgoroth spun around, its tiny eyes searching for the enemies.

But the beast was relentless. Even confused, it managed to catch Szeth with its massive tail, knocking him backwards.

In a flash of white, Szeth leaped back into the fray. He delivered a powerful flying kick to Kazgoroth's snout, distracting it for a moment longer.

"Now, Eryneth, before I become lunch!" Szeth yelled as he barely escaped a chomp from Kazgoroth's jaws.

Eryneth nodded and fired her bow. One by one, she loosed arrows into the ground around the beast. Each had been tied with elven rope, and they quickly tripped Kazgoroth in a tangle of rope. The beast roared in frustration, trying to free itself, but its own massive weight worked against it. It stumbled!

*Thud.* 

The enormous creature fell to its knees, the ground shaking.

"Ulfrik! This is our chance! Get to the sword!" Audrin yelled. The elf had finished his spell: a jet of pure magical energy shot from his staff into the Beastslayer. The blade, still next to Kazgoroth's feet, began to glow brightly.

Ulfrik was still too far away, struggling to cross the shaking ground near Kazgoroth. The beast was beginning to tear through Eryneth's nets. He steadied himself on a rock.

"Hold on tight!" Aknemeia yelled. She pointed her hands at the rock Ulfrik was leaning on, and a burst of fire exploded underneath it. The blast launched the rock—and its Northman rider—through the air toward the glowing sword.

Ulfrik's eyes went wide, but he managed to hit the ground rolling near the sword. He reached out and grabbed the Beastslayer. With a final roar of effort, the Northman stood and drove the magical blade straight upwards.

A blast of pure, white light burst from where the blade touched Kazgoroth, matching the purple-black energy pouring from the beast.

Kazgoroth let out a final, earth-shattering roar. Then, in an instant, the colossal monster burst into swirling, dark smoke and purple-black light. Then, the white light of the sword seemed to swallow the dark energy, and the monster was gone.

The howling wind stopped. The ground stopped shaking, and warm, bright sunlight streamed down into the Druid's Grove. 

A quiet, heavy silence fell over the clearing. The only sound was the tired breathing of the heroes. Every one of them was covered, head to toe, in dirt and soot.

From outside the grove, Prince Gregory peeked his head up over the large rock where he had taken cover. His eyes were wide, and his voice was quiet rather than pompous.

"Did... did we win?" the young prince asked quietly.

In the center of the grove, Ulfrik stood alone. The Beastslayer sword was still in his hand, but its glow had faded. The large man looked one-by-one at Aknemeia, Caryndal, Eryneth, and Szeth. After a long pause, Ulfrik spoke. "It... is done," he said, his voice raspy. "Thank you."

The Keepers looked at one another, took a deep breath, and smiled.
