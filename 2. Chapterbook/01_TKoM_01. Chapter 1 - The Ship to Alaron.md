# Chapter 1: The Ship to Alaron

The large wooden ship, *The Salty Clam*, rocked back and forth on the bright blue waves of the Sea of Swords. It was sailing to the island of Alaron. Four strangers sat on the wide deck. They didn’t know it yet, but they were about to become the best of friends.

First, there was Aknemeia. She was a tiefling, with pale skin, bright red hair, and two curved black horns on her head. Faint gold scales on her arms hinted at her mysterious past. She watched the sea quietly with golden eyes, preferring to keep to herself. Magic fire sparked playfully at her fingertips like little, colorful fireflies when she got lost in thought.

Next to her stood Eryneth, a wood elf whose pointed ears were visible beneath her pale white hair. Eryneth held a beautifully carved long wooden bow in her hands. She was a master at archery and surviving in the wild, but around other people she often felt shy and unsure of herself. Eryneth nervously adjusted her soft green cloak, trying her best to blend in.

On a tall barrel sat Szeth. He was a bald young human who wore plain white clothes and could move incredibly fast. Szeth always said exactly what he was thinking, even if it was a little rude or awkward. "This boat is very slow," Szeth announced loudly to no one in particular, crossing his arms.

Finally, there was Caryndal. He was a half-elf who wore armor and a green cloak. He loved playing his sweet-sounding lute and creating beautiful, theatrical illusions. With a graceful wave of his hand, a glowing butterfly fluttered around Szeth's head.

"Patience, speedy one," Caryndal chuckled. His voice was playful. "The journey is part of the fun!" They had been traveling on the sea for a few days now, but had hardly spoken. Each had been lost in their own thoughts.

A loud, complaining voice suddenly broke the silence. "Is there no food on this miserable tub that isn't completely covered in salt?" 

A young boy marched across the deck. He was wearing uncomfortably heavy, fancy velvet clothes that looked far too hot for a sea voyage. He stopped in front of the four strangers, pointing his nose high in the air. 

"I am Gregory," he announced, as if they should all be very impressed. "And I expect far better treatment than this. Someone go tell the captain to fetch me a fresh apple!"

"What you are is small and loud," Szeth noted unhelpfully. 

Gregory's face turned as red as his velvet coat, but before he could respond, a shout echoed from above.

The ship lookout called to the captain. "Sir, a ship is coming. It's the *Grey Wolf*! It's Northmen raiders!"

Suddenly, a loud *THUMP* shook the entire ship. Gregory tumbled backward into some wooden crates. He hit the deck with a yelp, but quickly scrambled to his feet, dusting off his coat with an annoyed huff.

Before any of them could tell him to be quiet, another shout echoed from the lookout. "Here they come!" 

The long Northmen ship had crashed up against the *Salty Clam*, and raiders were gathering on the other side. 

"Captain Ulfrik, we're ready to board!" a raider cheered from the other deck.

"For Norland!" yelled a big man with a large beard, as he jumped over to their ship. They could see from his clothes that he was the so-called Ulfrik, captain of the raiders. "Your ship is ours now!" he boomed with a loud laugh.

Gregory squeezed his eyes shut and covered his ears in fear. Ulfrik's eyes locked right onto him. "Ah, just who I was looking for!" the huge raider bellowed, scooping the boy up. 

"My mother won't let you get away with this!" Gregory yelled, struggling in the raider's grip.

The four strangers looked at each other, and then at the struggling boy. They didn't need to say a word. It was time to work together to save the ship and drive off the raiders.

"I will handle the big one," Szeth said, leaping off the barrel and flipping through the air. 

He landed right in front of the captain, trying to rescue the boy. Ulfrik grunted in surprise, stepping back but keeping a tight grip on the boy. "Leave this ship alone!" Aknemeia shouted, clapping her hands together.

A burst of bright red sparks zoomed at the raiders, making them cover their eyes. Eryneth drew her bow. *Twang! Twang!* She shot two arrows perfectly. They pinned the raiders' shirts right to the wooden mast of their ship!

The raiders wiggled, stuck in place. Caryndal laughed and played his lute. He cast a grand illusion. Suddenly, it looked like a giant, glowing sea monster was rising out of the water. "Sea monster, run!" yelled one raider. Others began to run away in panic.

Ulfrik snarled as his crew ran away. "This is too much trouble! Leave the treasures and get back to our ship! We have the boy!" he yelled. He swung back to his own ship, taking the boy with him. "Retreat!" The *Grey Wolf* ship quickly sailed away, leaving the four heroes standing together on the deck of the *Salty Clam*.

Eryneth lowered her bow, feeling a little braver but worried. Aknemeia gave Szeth a supportive nod. Caryndal played a serious, determined tune on his lute. The ship was safe from the raiders, but they felt there was something else going on. The four of them looked at each other.

"Well," said Caryndal. "I think we work together pretty well, don't you?"

"We have to go after him," Eryneth said quietly, her sharp elven eyes looking out at the retreating raiders. "That boy, Gregory. The raiders took him."

"He was very annoying, but I agree," said Szeth.

"Agreed," Aknemeia said, adjusting her cloak. "But where? They sailed off into the open ocean. We need a lead, and a plan."
