# Chapter 5: Arrival at Mintarn

It was late afternoon when the ship arrived at the busy port of Mintarn. The island was covered in wooden buildings and docks, with hundreds of ships rocking gently in the water. The salty sea breeze was mixed with the faint smells of smoke and sweet spices. 

Szeth stood at the front of the ship, looking out at the island. He took a deep breath as a group of sailors walked by. "This island smells like burnt fish and old shoes," he observed.

Caryndal quickly waved his hands, a colorful swirl of illusion magic sparkling around him as he stepped in front of Szeth. "Uh, what my friend meant to say is that it smells like... the rich aroma of adventure!" Caryndal declared theatrically to the passing sailors. He lowered his voice. "Please, Szeth. We are trying not to cause trouble right away."

They stepped off the ship and made their way through the busy streets. They passed a tavern with beautiful music coming from inside. "That sounds like a twostring!" Caryndal called to his friends. A quick look at the sign on the door confirmed his suspicions: beneath a picture of a string instrument, it read "The Twostring".

Before anyone could object, he pushed open the door.

Inside, it was cozy and welcoming. The tavern was filled with laughter and the sound of music. A friendly young man with pointed ears was playing a unique musical instrument on stage. It had a long wooden neck with two strings. He plucked at them as he told a wonderful story of elves, magic, and faeries.

After his song finished, the musician spotted them and came over to their table. "Welcome to The Twostring! I am Florian," he said, holding his instrument tightly. "I am the innkeeper here. But to be honest, I am having a very terrible day. You all look like the type who could help a pal out."

"Why?" asked Aknemeia, her golden eyes watching him carefully.

Instead of answering right away, Caryndal gently strummed his lute. He muttered a quick spell, and a tiny, sparkling illusion of a pixie began to dance on their table. "A beautiful song," Caryndal hummed. "It deserves a proper visual."

Florian gasped in delight. "I thought I recognized Fae magic. You have the touch of the faery realm... I knew I chose the right group. Please, you must listen to my story."

Florian sighed, looking around to make sure no one else was listening. "A gang of local thieves, run by Idruz, have taken my younger sister. My family back home is very wealthy, you see. Idruz found out, and her gang captured her for ransom! I have been trying to keep my family secret. I just want to enjoy playing my music here on Mintarn, but now my sister is in danger."

The heroes looked at each other.

"We will rescue her," Eryneth said, finding her courage.

"Yeah," replied Szeth. "We have a thing about rescuing captured people. It seems to be our favorite pastime."

"Excellent!" Caryndal clapped his hands together. "You can count on us! We are... The Sibling Savers!"

Szeth frowned. "That's no good either. We're not even her siblings, so it doesn't make sense."

Caryndal sighed. "Oh well. So... where is your sister, Florian?"

Florian pointed toward the docks. "They took her to one of the old warehouses near the water."

* * *

The heroes snuck out of the tavern and down to the docks as the sun began to set. They easily found the old warehouse, with several of Idruz's gruff-looking thieves standing guard outside. 

"What now?" whispered Aknemeia, conjuring a spark in her hand.

Caryndal grinned beneath his smooth white mask. "How about a performance?" he said as he strummed his lute. Suddenly, the loud, booming sound of marching boots echoed down the street. It sounded like an entire squad of angry town guards! He used his magic to make bright torches appear in the distance. 

Idruz's guards jumped in surprise. "Guards are coming! Hide!" one of them shouted. Most of the thieves scrambled away into the shadows.

Suddenly, the leader of the thieves kicked open the warehouse door. "Cowards! It's a trick!" she yelled at her fleeing guards. "I am Idruz, and you won't ruin my plans!"

She moved to go after the heroes, but Eryneth was too fast. She fired an arrow, knocking a heavy wooden sign off the wall above. It crashed down, knocking the bandit leader off balance. Before Idruz could recover, Aknemeia sent a bright spark of fire that made Idruz jump back in surprise.

In the moment of confusion, Szeth darted into the warehouse, quickly locating Florian's surprised sister. A short moment later, he dashed right out with her in tow.

"We're here with your brother," Szeth stated. "Trust us."

As the heroes regrouped and began to run, Idruz scrambled to her feet, shaking her fist at them. "This isn't over!" she howled. "I swear vengeance on all of you!" She tried to stumble after them, but the sparks from Aknemeia's spell and the rubble from the sign tripped her up.

As quickly as it had begun, it was over.

* * *

Back at The Twostring, Florian was overjoyed. He hugged his sister tightly. "Thank you! Thank you so much!" he cheered. "You are true heroes. How can I ever repay you?"

"We are looking for a pirate named Ulfrik," Aknemeia explained. "And a young boy named Gregory."

Florian's eyes widened into a smile. "I know someone who can help. I'll take you to see him tomorrow. For now, let's eat!"

* * *

The next morning, Florian took them to a tea shop, *The Jade Dragon*. 

"Allow me to introduce you to Malthos," Florian said, opening the door of the shop to reveal walls covered in tea jars of all shapes and sizes. "He knows everything that happens on Mintarn."

Behind the counter of the shop was an older tiefling man with red skin, long curved horns, and keen eyes. Malthos looked closely at the group, his gaze stopping on Aknemeia. He smiled a small, knowing smile.

"Good to see you again, young one," Malthos said softly. "I see you are walking a different path from your father."

"Malthos?" said Aknemeia, surprised. "You remember me? It has been so long since you left my father's... organization."

"I remember much, young lady, and I'm glad to see that you are well," Malthos said softly. "Perhaps we can catch up later. For now, Florian tells me you are in a hurry."

Aknemeia stood tall, and a spark glowed in her eyes. "Yes," she said. "We're looking for a pirate named Ulfrik."

Malthos nodded. "Ulfrik is well known here. He and his men recently passed through here with a young boy. They were headed deep into the Great Forest."

"Oh," said Eryneth, her eyes lighting up with excitement. "I suppose we're off to the woods!"

Just like that, the mission to save Prince Gregory was back on track.
