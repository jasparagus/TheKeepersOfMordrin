# Chapter 6: The Great Forest

The heroes left the chaos and noise of Mintarn behind and stepped into the edge of the Great Forest. The trees quickly became denser and more massive, with thick trunks covered in bright green moss and twisting vines. The air was heavy and filled with the strange sounds of chirping insects, bird calls, and rustling leaves. 

Eryneth took a deep breath, a bright smile spreading across her face. After spending so much time in crowded, loud towns and on brightly lit, rocking ships, she felt completely at home among the quiet trees. It was peaceful, much like the forests she remembered from her childhood. 

She confidently took the lead, guiding the group through the dense bushes and pointing out the broken branches and deep footprints left behind by Ulfrik and his pirate crew. 

"They went this way," Eryneth said softly, pointing to a patch of flattened ferns. "There are many heavy boots here. And... smaller prints. Someone was dragging their feet. I think it is the young Prince Gregory."

"How do you do that, Eryneth?" Aknemeia asked, her golden eyes scanning the forest floor in confusion.

"My mother taught me," Eryneth said with a smile. "Where I come from, in the High Forest, we learn to listen to the trees."

"Wait, you can talk to trees, too?" Szeth asked, surprised. "What's that one named?"

"No," Eryneth chuckled. "I can read the signs of the woods. It's a skill that takes years of practice to master."

 "Let's keep moving," Aknemeia said. "We've got to catch up to Ulfrik and his crew."

They followed the trail deeper into the woods, keeping their eyes peeled for any signs of danger. Suddenly, a loud *SNAP* echoed through the trees!

A hidden net made of thick, brittle vines shot up from the forest floor beneath Szeth's feet. But the tall warrior was too quick. With incredible speed, Szeth deftly backflipped out of the net's path, landing gracefully on his feet as the flimsy net snapped harmlessly in the air next to him.

Caryndal clapped his hands in delight. "That is some nimble footwork, my friend."

Szeth brushed a bit of dirt off his shoulder and just shrugged. "It was a very poorly made trap. We made better nets back in my village. But thank you."

Before they could take another step, the branches above them began to shake wildly. *Rustle! Crash!*

A large group of goblins suddenly dropped from the trees, surrounding the heroes in a tight circle! They were small, green creatures with long, pointy ears, and big eyes. They wore oversized clothing that looked like stitched-together rags covered in mud.

They weren't particularly scary, but made up for it in enthusiasm as they shook crude wooden spears and pointed excitedly at Caryndal’s beautiful lute and Eryneth's elegant elven cloak.

"Shiny things! We take the shiny things!" one of the goblins shrieked, bouncing up and down. 

Caryndal quickly stepped forward, raising his hands with a warm, welcoming smile. A colorful swirl of illusion magic sparkled around his fingers, creating floating, dancing lights in the air to distract the wild horde. 

"Greetings, tiny woodland friends!" Caryndal announced theatrically, bowing low to the ground. "There is no need for violence. We are just passing through! We are... The Forest Friends!"

The goblins stopped and stared at the swirling magical lights for a moment. Then, the leader of the goblins hissed angrily and threw a sloppy handful of wet mud right at Caryndal’s face. *Splat!*

Eryneth grimaced as Caryndal slowly wiped the mud away, sighing deeply. "Well," Eryneth commented with a nervous laugh. "I don't think that name is going to work."

With that, the goblins charged! It was a fast-paced scramble! The little creatures ran about, poking at the heroes and trying to snatch their belongings. They jumped and tumbled around, pulling on Szeth's cloak and trying to climb on Caryndal's back to reach his lute. Szeth dashed out of the way, wrestling a few of the goblins away from Caryndal.

"That's enough of that, now get back!" Aknemeia shouted in a magically booming voice. She raised her hands dramatically. A jet of fire shot from her fingertips, blasting a nearby rotten log. *BOOM!*

A loud explosion echoed through the clearing, making the goblins shriek in fear. They began to jump up and down, covering their large ears.

With the goblins distracted, Caryndal strummed a fast-paced, energetic tune on his lute. Magic flowed from his strings, dancing around Eryneth. Her green eyes sharpened as the magic swirled around her. With supernatural speed, she loosed an arrow. *Thwip*. And another *Thwip*. Each arrow flew true, and one by one, the goblins found themselves pinned to the thick tree trunks around the clearing by their oversized, baggy tunics. 

Realizing they were trapped and outmatched, the goblins threw down their wooden spears and raised their hands in the air. 

"We surrender! We surrender to the sword thieves!" the leader of the goblins cried out, dangling helplessly from a low branch by his tunic. He wiggled and kicked his short legs.

Aknemeia stopped, tilting her head in pure confusion. She lowered her glowing hands. "Sword thieves? What are you talking about? We didn't steal any swords."

"Yes, you did!" the goblin huffed, crossing his little arms. "Big humans came through here yesterday! They took our special shiny sword! The Beastslayer! Them and that loud, complaining boy!"

The heroes looked at each other, realizing what was going on. 

"They think we're with Ulfrik," Eryneth whispered, "and that 'loud boy' must be Prince Gregory."

"I don't even have a beard," Szeth said, crossing his arms and looking at the dangling goblins. "We are not with those thieves," he said flatly. "And even if we were, we wouldn't steal a sword from a bunch of stinky tree-gremlins who don't even know how to tie a proper trap knot."

The goblins gasped loudly in offense. Caryndal gasped too, quickly stepping in before Szeth could make them angry enough to try fighting again.

"What my friend means is," Caryndal said quickly, waving his hands, "we are hunting the men who stole your sword! If you tell us which way they went, we will let you down."

The goblin leader squinted his eyes, thinking for a moment. Then, he pointed a long, green finger deeper into the dark woods. "They went that way, toward the spooky rocks! The old druid place in the middle of the forest!"

"Thank you," Caryndal said with a polite, sweeping bow. He gave Szeth a look that said, *Be nice.*

Eryneth stepped forward and carefully pulled her arrows out of the tree trunks, letting the grumpy goblins drop safely to the mossy ground. As the goblins quickly scrambled away into the bushes, muttering to themselves, the heroes gathered together. They each took a deep, focused breath, and then they set off in the direction provided by the goblins, venturing even deeper into the shadows of the Great Forest.
