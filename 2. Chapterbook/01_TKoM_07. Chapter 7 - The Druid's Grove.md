# Chapter 7: The Druid's Grove

The heroes crept carefully through the thick brush of the Great Forest. The trees grew older and the shadows grew deeper until suddenly, the woods opened up into a wide, circular clearing. 

This was the ancient Druid's Grove, just like the goblins had said. Giant, moss-covered stone pillars stood in a wide circle, looking like sleepy stone giants watching over the forest. The air felt heavy and tingling with ancient magic.

"Stay low," whispered Szeth, dropping low behind a massive fallen log. 

The rest of the heroes quickly ducked behind the log, peering over the rough bark to spy on the center of the clearing.

Standing right in the center of the stone circle was Ulfrik, the fierce captain who had attacked their ship. He looked strong and intense, holding a beautiful, gleaming silver sword in his hands—the stolen Beastslayer. 

Beside him stood an older, serious-looking elven man wearing long, flowing robes. He held a wooden staff that hummed with magical energy.

"That must be Audrin," whispered Caryndal. "The one who helped Alicia trap Kazgoroth in the Shards."

Sitting on a flat stone right between them was none other than Prince Gregory.

"And there's our prince," Szeth grumbled quietly. "He's whining as much as usual."

He wasn't tied up, and he didn't look scared at all. Instead, he looked incredibly annoyed. 

"This rock is cold," Prince Gregory complained loudly, crossing his arms and pouting. He seemed completely oblivious to the danger he was in. "And my boots are muddy! I demand a proper velvet chair and a plate of warm cheese tarts!"

Ulfrik rolled his eyes and ignored the boy, turning to the man in the robes. "Are you sure this is going to work, Audrin? Is the boy going to be enough to summon the beast?"

Audrin nodded slowly, his face grim. He began drawing strange symbols in the dirt around Gregory with his staff. "Yes, Ulfrik. Queen Alicia used her royal bloodline to bind Kazgoroth into the Shards long ago. Because Gregory is her son, we can use him as a magical conduit to draw the beast's spirit out of the Shards and pull it back into the physical world."

"And then," Ulfrik said, running a thumb along the flat edge of the Beastslayer sword, "I will slay it."

"When Queen Alicia, Suhlur, Keane, and I first trapped Kazgoroth, we did not have this sword to defeat him," Audrin said, pointing his staff at the glowing blade. "With this sword, we can finally banish it once and for all."

Prince Gregory stopped complaining about cheese tarts and looked up, his eyes wide. "Wait. You're trying to summon a giant, evil monster right here? Next to me? Isn't that dangerous? What if we fail?"

Ulfrik looked down at the young prince. His eyes were fierce but sad. "The risk is necessary to bring justice for my people," the Northman said firmly. "Kazgoroth's lies tore my people apart. I will not let it hide in a crystal waiting for a chance to escape."

Behind the fallen log, the heroes listened in shock. 

Aknemeia grabbed Caryndal's sleeve. "This is terrible!" she whispered. "Summoning Kazgoroth? On purpose? There's no way that's a good idea!"

"She's right," Caryndal said, his voice matching the dim light of the grove. "And if they fail to defeat it, Kazgoroth will be free to cause chaos across the whole island. We can't let them finish this ritual."

Eryneth peered through a gap in the stones. Her keen elven eyes scanned the clearing. "There are too many open spaces. But look over there—the tall grass near that pillar. I can sneak around and get close to the prince."

"Good idea," Caryndal said, reaching for his lute. "I will distract them. Something loud and confusing to draw their attention."

Aknemeia stood up slightly, golden sparks dancing across her knuckles. "And I'll give them a fireworks show."

Szeth nodded, tensing up like a coiled spring. "And I'll grab the boy while they are distracted."

They traded determined nods. It was time to stop a summoning, save a whiny prince, and hopefully, not get squashed by a giant monster in the process.
