# Chapter 8: The Ritual Interrupted

The heroes huddled behind the massive fallen log, finalizing their daring rescue plan. Eryneth was fixing the string on her bow, and Caryndal was tuning his lute when Audrin stepped towards Prince Gregory in the center of the Druid's Circle. 

To the Keepers' surprise, the elven wizard pulled out a dark, pulsing crystal. It was another Shard of Kazgoroth. 

"Wait, what?" Aknemeia hissed quietly. "Is he supposed to have one of those?"

"Didn't the queen say Audrin helped her trap Kazgoroth? This must be the last shard..." Caryndal trailed off, his eyes going wide as Audrin set the shard on the ground beside Prince Gregory.

With a loud crack that seemed to echo through the entire forest, Audrin smashed his staff into the shard. The crystal shattered, and the clearing filled with thick black fog and black lightning. The dark energy spun into a vortex, reaching out like smoky fingers toward Prince Gregory in the center of the stone circle. The sky above the ancient clearing rapidly darkened, and the wind began to howl fiercely. 

"I think that's our cue!" Caryndal shouted over the roaring wind.

He leaped up onto the fallen log, playing a frantic tune on his lute. Green and gold magical notes exploded from the strings, zipping over to Eryneth and Szeth. The empowered heroes dashed away, alight with magical power.

"I've got the guards!" Aknemeia yelled, standing up. She raised her hands to the dark sky and unleashed a dazzling barrage of magical flames. Explosions erupted throughout the clearing, followed by a shower of sticky, glowing magical webs that rained down, trapping and confusing Ulfrik’s guards.

The clearing descended into total chaos, but in the center of the storm, Audrin did not flinch. He tightened his grip on his staff, his eyes locked on the swirling vortex, and continued casting the dangerous spell.

"You again?" Ulfrik growled, spotting the heroes through the black smoke and flying spells. He sliced a magical web from the air using the silver Beastslayer sword. "I must admit, your persistence is impressive! But this cannot be stopped. It has to be done!"

"We'll see about that!" Szeth roared, dashing past Ulfrik before the large Northman could react. 

Ignoring the dire warnings, the white-clad hero charged headfirst through the magical storm. Eryneth ran with him along the edge of the grove, moving with practiced grace and enchanted speed. The instant a guard escaped from Aknemeia’s webs, one of Eryneth's arrows pinned their cloak to the ground or knocked their weapon from their hand, halting them in their tracks.

Szeth weaved through the chaos until he reached the center of the stone circle where Prince Gregory was sitting, surrounded by black smoke. The prince, no longer looking bored, was wide-eyed. 

"Help!" he yelled, as the vortex of magic swirled around him. Black tendrils of smoke seemed to grab at the prince. In a flash of white, Szeth delivered a powerful kick at the solid smoke, causing the tendrils to break apart. The nimble hero grabbed the boy's collar and yanked him out of the swirling magic. 

"You're coming with me," Szeth stated. For once, Gregory did not whine.

Just as Gregory left the circle, a portion of the swirling black magic formed into a massive reptile tail, smashing into the ground where Gregory had been. 

*BOOM!* 

The ground shook, throwing Szeth, Eryneth, and the prince tumbling into the tall grass.

With the prince removed as the magical conduit, the spell completely lost control. The howling wind intensified into a deafening roar. Unfiltered dark energy poured relentlessly from the broken Shard, tearing a jagged, glowing hole in the space between worlds. 

The swirling black magic rapidly grew, taking on a terrifying physical shape right before their eyes. 

In a final burst of blinding purple energy, Kazgoroth fully materialized. It was no longer just a spirit trapped in a crystal—it was a real, colossal, corrupted beast. It looked like a towering, nightmare version of a T-Rex, with thick, scaly armor and eyes that burned with green fire. 

The demigod unleashed an ear-splitting, thunderous roar that shook the remaining leaves from the ancient trees and knocked everyone in the clearing flat onto the muddy ground. 

As the dust slowly cleared, coughing and groaning filled the grove. The Keepers of Mordrin, the scattered raiders, and the trembling prince all stared up at the impossible, nightmarish beast towering over them. 

Knowing the demigod could not be allowed to escape into the world, Audrin and Ulfrik pushed themselves up from the dirt. They exchanged a single, desperate look with the heroes. This was no longer about a kidnapped prince; this was about saving the entire island.

Ulfrik raised the glowing silver Beastslayer sword high into the air. "For the Northmen!" he roared, his voice filled with fierce determination.

"For the Queen!" Audrin shouted, readying his glowing staff. 

Together, the two former enemies charged bravely toward the towering beast. 

Behind the fallen log, Caryndal, Aknemeia, Eryneth, and Szeth shared a brief, determined nod. They had started this wild adventure to rescue a prince, but they were the Keepers of Mordrin—they weren't going to run away from a fight, not even one against a giant, magical T-Rex.

Raising their weapons, the heroes joined the charge against Kazgoroth.
