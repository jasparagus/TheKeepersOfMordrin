# Chapter 2: Arrival at Carraigport

The *Salty Clam* bumped gently against the wooden docks of Carraigport. They had made it. But the victory felt hollow.

The town of Carraigport was small and bustling, smelling strongly of fish, salt, and wet wood. Gulls cried overhead as sailors unloaded crates from docked ships. The group made their way through the busy morning market, their stomachs rumbling, until they found a cozy tavern called The Hefty Pint. Inside, a roaring fire crackled in the hearth, and a plump, cheerful man was wiping down tables. 

"Welcome, travelers!" the innkeeper called out. "I'm Silas. Best breakfast in Carraigport, guaranteed!"

Szeth sniffed the air, unblinking. "It smells like burned porridge. And your town is very small. It lacks tactical defensive walls."

Silas blinked, his cheerful smile freezing. 

"What my friend means," Caryndal jumped in smoothly, flashing a dazzling smile, "is that we are simply overwhelmed by your town's quaint, rustic charm! We'll take four bowls of your finest, slightly-charred porridge, please."

As they sat down to eat, they kept their voices low, discussing the raiders and the missing boy. But before they could come up with a plan, an elderly man approached their table. He wore fine clothes that looked a little too big for him, and he had deep worry lines etched into his forehead. Behind him stood a nervous-looking man in robes.

"Excuse me," the man said, wringing his hands. "Are you the heroes who fought off Ulfrik's raiders? The whole dock is buzzing about you."

"Indeed, we are the *Clamshell Avengers*," Caryndal announced, standing and taking a dramatic bow.

Szeth looked flatly at Caryndal. "That's a terrible name."

"I... I am Laird Marvyn, the leader of this town," the man said, looking confused. "And this is my advisor, Tychon. We need your help. Desperately."

"We're already on a mission," Aknemeia said, her golden eyes narrowing. "We're looking for a captured boy."

Laird Marvyn's eyes widened. "The boy? The one the raiders captured? We may be able to help you track him down. Also, we can pay you."

The heroes leaned in with interest.

"I can tell you everything," Laird Marvyn promised, lowering his voice. "But we truly need your help. My town is in danger. Every night, we have been hearing spooky sounds coming from the edge of town, and people have been disappearing. We are out of our depth."

Szeth crossed his arms. "I guess you're probably too old to do it yourself."

Caryndal sighed loudly. "Szeth, please have some tact." He turned back to the Laird with a smile. "Where are these sounds coming from?"

"An old farm on the edge of the woods," Tychon, the advisor, chimed in nervously. "It belongs to a rich settler named Suhlur. He used to be friendly with the townsfolk, but he hasn't spoken to anyone in a long time, and everyone is too scared by the noises. Please, will you investigate?"

"We have a deal," Eryneth agreed firmly.

* * *

The walk to Suhlur's property took them through a patch of dense woods. By the time they reached the old farm, the sun was beginning to set, casting long shadows across the ground. 

Suhlur's house sat in the middle of an overgrown field. It was totally silent. 

"This place feels... wrong," Aknemeia whispered, snapping her fingers. A small flame danced on her thumb, casting a warm glow in the twilight. 

The front door was locked tight, but a quick shove from Szeth popped it open with a loud *CRACK*. The heroes stepped inside, coughing as dust swirled around them. The house was a mess. Strange symbols were scratched into the floorboards, and the air smelled like old, stale earth. Eryneth quickly spotted a messy desk in the corner. Scattered across it were notes covered in magical writing and a hand-drawn map. 

"Look," Eryneth pointed to the map. "There's a clearing marked here. Just behind the house."

Suddenly, a loud *THUMP* echoed from the shadows. 

A figure lurched out from the dark hallway. It was a zombie, shuffling and groaning as it walked towards them.

"Zombie!" Caryndal said, grabbing his lute and strumming a chord. Magical energy flowed from the lute strings, protecting his friends.

The zombie lunged, its arms outstretched. But the heroes were ready. 

Szeth darted forward, faster than lightning, and delivered a spinning kick to the zombie. Aknemeia sent a bright blast of fire that singed the zombie's ragged cloak. Before the creature could recover, Eryneth smoothly drew an arrow, aimed, and fired. 

*THWACK!* 

The arrow pinned the zombie's sleeve firmly to the thick wooden doorframe. It groaned, wiggling helplessly against the wood, unable to break free. The fight was over in seconds.

"Well," Caryndal said, dusting off his hands. "That was delightfully spooky, but not very effective."

"Suhlur left it here on purpose," Aknemeia said, inspecting the magical notes on the desk. "He's been making these spooky zombies with dark magic."

"If there's one here," Eryneth said perfectly calmly, pointing out the window toward the backyard, "there might be more in that barn."

* * *

The barn stood at the edge of the property, its enormous wooden doors shut tight. The spooky sounds Laird Marvyn had mentioned were much louder now. Muffled groans and the rattling of wood drifted out from inside.

Eryneth carefully pushed the heavy barn doors open. The hinges squealed. 

Inside, the barn didn't hold cows or horses. Instead, there were rows of large, wooden cages. Inside the cages, dozens of shadowy figures shuffled and groaned. It was a whole room full of zombies!

"Suhlur hasn't just been making one zombie," Caryndal whispered, his eyes wide. "He's making an army!"

"This is bad," Aknemeia said, her hands sparking with nervous energy.

"Then we must destroy them," Szeth said, his fists raising into a fighting stance. "Tactically."

"No," Eryneth said, putting a hand on his shoulder. "They aren't going anywhere right now. We need to stop the person making them. We need to find Suhlur."

Caryndal puffed out his chest and smiled, a sudden glint in his eye. He waved his hands in a wide, sweeping motion. With a burst of fey magic, a shimmering, glowing banner appeared in the air above their heads. It crackled with bright, colorful sparkles, illuminating the dark barn with the words "Clamshell Avengers".

"Fear not, citizens of Carraigport!" Caryndal declared dramatically, pitching his voice so it echoed through the barn. "Your rescuers are here! We will find this dark wizard, defeat his army, and save the day! And then—" he looked at the others, his smile turning serious, "—we get our answers."

"That name is still terrible," said Szeth. But he stood proud anyway. They had a dark wizard to catch, and a prince to save.

The heroes stood together as the light of Caryndal's magic lit the barn. Suddenly Eryneth pointed. "There!"
