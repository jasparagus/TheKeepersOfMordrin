# Chapter 3: The Mad Wizard

Eryneth had spotted footprints in the dirt. The group followed her, while Aknemeia's flickering magic fire lit the way. "How can you all see in this dark?" Szeth complained. 

"Elves can see perfectly fine," Eryneth explained. 

"Tieflings, too," said Aknemeia. 

"Half-elf," said Caryndal, shrugging, "on my father's side."

Szeth frowned, but followed them anyway. He only stubbed his toe once in the dark.

The trail led out of the barn and to a hidden trapdoor. Beneath it, a set of stone stairs led down into a basement. Dust coated the steps, and the air smelled heavy with stale dirt and strange magic. 

"Stay close," Eryneth whispered, keeping an arrow notched in her bow. "Whatever is making those zombies is down here."

Aknemeia hissed, pointing at the glowing light at the bottom of the stairs. "Look!"

They crept into a wide, dusty basement that looked like a laboratory. Bubbling potions simmered in glass jars, and magical trinkets littered long wooden tables. In the center of the room stood an old man in black robes. He clutched a dark, twisted wooden staff with a black crystal on it. On his shoulder sat a ragged raven zombie. 

"Who dares enter!" the old man shouted, his voice cracking. He pointed his staff at them. "I am Suhlur! My master, Kazgoroth, does not tolerate your meddling!"

The raven let out a loud *CAW!* and flew straight at Caryndal!

"I don't think so!" Caryndal called out. He quickly strummed his fingers on his lute, casting a brilliant spell. With a loud *POP*, a glowing, magical cage appeared in the air around the raven. 

*Squawk!* The raven bounced harmlessly off the magical bars, trapped and defeated in an instant. 

"Mordrin! No!" Suhlur wailed, looking at his trapped bird. Then, his eyes narrowed, and the magical energies in the room began to swirl. "You will pay for that!" 

"Your posture is terrible, old wizard!" Szeth yelled. He leaped across the room, far faster than typical humans, dodging a spark of green magic that Suhlur fired. Szeth landed perfectly in front of the wizard and delivered a swift, powerful kick right to the wooden staff.

Suhlur stumbled backward, trying to cast another spell, but Aknemeia was ready. She slammed her hands together, throwing a ball of bright, warm fire that collided with Suhlur’s green magic, dissolving it with a loud *SIZZLE*. 

"Now, Eryneth!" Caryndal shouted.

Eryneth drew her bow back and fired. Her arrow sailed through the air and hit Suhlur’s dark staff perfectly in the middle. 

*CRACK!* 

The staff shattered into a dozen pieces. One of them was a chunk of black, jagged crystal. It clattered to the stone floor.

Then, the dark green magical lights in the basement vanished. Suhlur gasped, and his eyes widened. He slumped backward onto the floor, all the fight leaving him instantly. 

Aknemeia slowly walked toward the broken shards of the staff. The black crystal pulsed with cold energy. It seemed to whisper to Aknemeia, offering her dark power. She hesitated a moment, but Aknemeia shook her head. She took out a thick cloth and wrapped it tightly inside. The whispering immediately stopped.

"I think the spell is broken," Aknemeia sighed in relief. 

From upstairs, a massive commotion broke out in the barn. The spooky zombie groans had been replaced by loud talking.

"The magic!" Caryndal cheered. "Without the staff, the zombies have turned back into normal people!"

Suhlur looked up at the heroes, tears welling in his tired eyes. He didn't look like a mad wizard anymore. He just looked like a sad, exhausted old man. 

"You did it," Suhlur rasped, pointing a shaky finger at the wrapped stone in Aknemeia's hands. "You broke its hold on me. Thank you."

"Why were you making zombies, Suhlur?" Eryneth asked gently, lowering her bow. 

"It was the Shard," Suhlur explained quietly, leaning heavily on his desk. "Long ago, the great High Queen Alicia defeated a terrible beast named Kazgoroth. I was one of the Queen's trusted friends who helped her. She asked me to keep that Shard safe, and hide its dark magic from the world. But... it was too strong. Over the years, it twisted my thoughts towards evil. Without you, I would have been lost forever."

"The Queen did that?" Caryndal said in awe.

Suhlur nodded slowly, his eyes heavy. "Yes. It ended a long conflict with the Northmen, but that is a story for another time. For now, the Shard of Kazgoroth must be secured. We must contact the Queen. It is far too dangerous." He breathed heavily. "I am... so very tired."

Within seconds, the old wizard was fast asleep, snoring softly in his chair. 

"He owes us answers when he wakes," Eryneth said, looking at the bundle in Aknemeia's hands. "This seems important."

"And what about Gregory?" Szeth asked crossing his arms. "The captured boy."

Caryndal adjusted his cloak, striking a heroic pose. "Everyone, relax. We just won the day! We need a name before we return to town. The Dazzling Defenders? The Fearsome Four? You didn't like Clamshell Avengers."

"The Danger Punchers," Szeth suggested. "Because we punch danger."

"A name can wait," Eryneth said. "For now, we have to return to Laird Marvyn."
