# Review of Book 1: The Keepers of Mordrin

This document contains a review of the first chapterbook (Chapters 1-10) for internal consistency, typos, plot gaps, and loose threads. It serves as a planning document to aid in proofreading and preparing for the sequel.

## Plot Gaps & Continuity Issues
- **The Raven 'Mordrin' (Chapter 10 vs Chapter 3):** In Chapter 10, Szeth suddenly pulls a cage containing Mordrin the raven out of his bag, leading to the group's name. However, the raven was last seen in Chapter 3 trapped in a magical cage by Caryndal. It is never mentioned that Szeth acquired a physical cage, retrieved the bird, or carried it for the rest of the adventure. This is a noticeable continuity gap that feels contrived.
    - *Idea (Chapter 3):* Pivot Szeth's morbid "monster collector" trait into a kid-friendly "weird pet collector" running gag to smoothly seed the raven's presence. After the magic fades, Mordrin turns back into a normal, grumpy raven. Szeth dumps it into an empty wooden lantern box from the basement, stating he is starting a "dangerous beast collection".
    - *Idea (Chapter 4):* During the boat ride to Mintarn, include a brief joke where Szeth complains the ship's biscuits aren't good enough for his "fearsome beast." Caryndal sighs and reminds him it's just a regular raven now.
    - *Idea (Chapters 7-8):* When rescuing the prince, Szeth could complain that Gregory is heavier (or more annoying) than his birdcage. By seeing Szeth carry it throughout the journey, the reveal in Chapter 10 plays as a satisfying payoff.
- **The Return Journey (Chapter 10):** The transition from the middle of the Great Forest (Druid's Grove) to having tea in Mintarn (The Jade Dragon) is handled with a simple scene break. The journey back with the Prince is completely skipped, causing a jarring transition.
    - *Idea (The "Tired Trek" Montage):* Add a short paragraph right after Ulfrik leaves to describe the exhausting but victorious walk back through the dark woods. Gregory complains about his muddy velvet coat, while Szeth complains about how heavy his new birdcage is. [Jasper note: I dislike this. Gregory is somewhat changed, and I don't want to emphasize that they're exhausted.]
    - *Idea (The Goblin Escort):* The Keepers bump into the goblins from Chapter 6, who saw the giant monster explode from afar. Awestruck by the heroes, the goblins eagerly guide them out of the forest on a hidden, much faster shortcut. [Amusing, but adds too much length.]
    - *Idea (The Welcome Back):* Instead of cutting directly to sitting inside the Jade Dragon, show the heroes breaking through the treeline at dawn. Florian sees them returning covered in mud and eagerly ushers them inside for a well-deserved breakfast and tea with Malthos. [I like this best]

## Loose Threads (Possible Sequel Hooks)
- **Idruz's Vengeance (Chapter 5):** The bandit leader Idruz swears vengeance on the Keepers ("This isn't over! I swear vengeance on all of you!"), but she is not seen again in this book. This is an excellent hook to reserve for Book 2.
- **Florian's Sister (Chapter 5):** The Keepers rescue Florian's sister, but she is never given a name in the text.
- **The Beastslayer Sword (Chapter 10):** Ulfrik gifts the heroes the ancient Beastslayer sword. They now have a powerful artifact in their possession, which should be accounted for in the next book.
- **Aknemeia's Father (Chapter 10):** Aknemeia mentions she's in no hurry to get back to her father in Baldur's Gate, establishing her personal arc for a future book.
- **Szeth's Quest (Chapter 10):** Szeth asks about finding a bad guy with a big black sword, clearly setting up his ongoing personal quest.

## Typos & Formatting Errors
- **Chapter 4:** Contains a double space. -> `"We don't even  want it."`
- **Chapter 10:** Contains a stray quotation mark at the end of Prince Gregory's dialogue paragraph. -> `"he still complained about his damp socks and the lack of fine cheese."`
- **Chapter 7 (Potential Outline Conflict):** The Chapterbook outline specifically advises to "avoid saying blood" (use lineage instead) when discussing the summoning ritual. Chapter 7 uses the term "royal bloodline". While not overly graphic, it skirts very closely to the target audience restriction and could be adjusted to "royal lineage".
