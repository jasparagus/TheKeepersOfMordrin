# Chapter 10: The Keepers of Mordrin

The silence in the Druid's Grove was almost as loud as Kazgoroth's roar had been just moments before. In the center of the clearing, Ulfrik still held the legendary Beastslayer sword, its silver blade gleaming in the afternoon sunlight. The giant reptilian monster was gone, banished back into the dark shards from whence it came.

Around the edges of the grove, Aknemeia, Caryndal, Eryneth, and Szeth slowly lowered their weapons. They were covered in dirt, soot, and mud, but they were victorious. Prince Gregory, looking surprisingly small and disheveled, peeked out from behind a large mossy rock.

Ulfrik finally broke the silence. He drove the tip of the Beastslayer into the soft earth and turned to face the four heroes. 

"I have fought alongside many warriors," the large Northman rumbled, his voice echoing in the quiet grove. "But I have never seen such bravery and skill as you showed today. You have my gratitude."

Audrin, the elven wizard, stepped up beside Ulfrik and bowed his head respectfully. "It is true. Without your magic and quick thinking, Kazgoroth would have destroyed us all."

Caryndal rested his lute on his hip, brushing a leaf from his shoulder with a dramatic flair. "Well, rescuing princes and battling ancient monsters is thirsty work, but we are professionals, after all."

Eryneth rolled her eyes playfully but smiled. "We did what had to be done. But..." She narrowed her eyes at Ulfrik. "Why did you do all of this? Kidnapping the prince, summoning a terrifying beast? That seems like a terrible plan!"

"It was the *only* plan," Ulfrik replied, his expression turning serious. He gestured to the prince. "Kazgoroth could not simply be defeated in battle. It had to be banished permanently using its own dark magic against it. To do that, we needed three things: this sword, Audrin's dark crystal shard, and the magical connection of Gregory's royal lineage."

"So you used the prince as bait?" Szeth asked crossing his arms. As always, the white-clad warrior refused to sugarcoat his words. "That is terrible strategy. High Queen Alicia will have your head for it."

"Exactly," Audrin sighed, looking down. "The Queen would never have agreed to put her son in such danger, even to save the world. We had to work in secret. It was a terrible risk, but it was necessary."

Aknemeia crossed her arms, tiny sparks of fire dancing on her fingertips. "It was reckless! You could have gotten everyone destroyed!"

The atmosphere in the grove grew tense. For a long moment, the heroes and the Northman simply stared at one another. Finally, Ulfrik nodded slowly. 

"Perhaps you are right," Ulfrik conceded. "But the deed is done, and the world is safe." He walked over to Gregory and gently nudged the boy toward the heroes. "Take the prince home. We must return to our ship." As he turned away, the pirate captain paused and looked back over his shoulder. "My people are in your debt."

With that, Ulfrik and Audrin gathered their remaining men and vanished into the dense Mintarn Great Forest.

The journey back to Carraigport was long but surprisingly peaceful. After facing a giant, magical T-Rex, a trek through the woods felt like a casual stroll. Even Prince Gregory seemed changed. He was much quieter now, though he still occasionally complained about the mud on his boots and the lack of fine cheeses in their travel rations.

"I imagine my mother will throw a magnificent feast upon my return," Gregory announced as they finally spotted the bustling docks of Carraigport in the distance. 

"She had better," Caryndal laughed. "I haven't had a proper meal since we met you!"

When the group finally reached the royal palace, they were immediately rushed into the grand throne room. High Queen Alicia Kendrick leaped from her throne and sprinted down the steps, pulling her son into a tight, tearful embrace. 

"Gregory!" she cried. "Oh, my sweet boy! I thought I had lost you forever!"

"Mother, please!" Gregory grumbled, his cheeks turning bright red. "I'm perfectly fine. These... individuals brought me back."

The Queen wiped her tears and turned to the four heroes. Her royal expression was replaced with one of profound gratitude. 

"You have done the impossible," High Queen Alicia said smoothly. "You tracked down the most dangerous pirates on the sea, rescued my son, and helped defeat an ancient evil. The Kendrick family—and the entire world—owes you a debt we can never repay. From this day forward, you have a lasting alliance with our kingdom."

That evening, the palace threw a celebration grander than anything the heroes had ever seen. The tables were piled high with roasted meats, fresh fruits, and, to Gregory's delight, the absolute finest cheeses in the land. Musicians played lively tunes in the corner, though Caryndal made sure to pull out his lute and show them how it was *really* done.

Later in the evening, the four friends found themselves standing together on a balcony, overlooking the twinkling lights of the city below. The night air was cool and refreshing.

"So," Eryneth said softly, looking at her companions. "We rescued the prince. What comes next?"

Aknemeia smiled, her fiery purple eyes sparkling in the moonlight. "Whatever it is, we'll do it together. We make a pretty good team."

"Speaking of that," Caryndal interrupted, striking a heroic pose against the stone railing. "A great team needs a great name! The Clamshell Avengers? The Shard Snatchers? The Forest Friends?"

Szeth simply shook his head. "Those are terrible. Absolutely awful."

"Well, do you have a better idea?" Caryndal challenged.

Szeth looked out over the city for a moment, thinking back to the very beginning of their journey. He remembered the dark raven, Mordrin, that had attacked them on the ship, setting all of these events in motion.

"The Keepers of Mordrin," Szeth stated plainly. "It sounds strong. Protective."

The others exchanged a look. 

"I like it," Eryneth agreed, a wide smile spreading across her face. 

Aknemeia nodded enthusiastically. "The Keepers of Mordrin. It has a nice ring to it."

"Fine," Caryndal conceded, though he secretly thought it was brilliant, too. "The Keepers of Mordrin it is!"

As they stood there beneath the stars, the four heroes knew this was only the beginning. They had started as strangers on a ship, but they had become an unbreakable found family. Whatever dangers or adventures awaited them, the Keepers of Mordrin would face them together.
