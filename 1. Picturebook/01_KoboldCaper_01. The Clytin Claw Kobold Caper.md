# The Keepers of Mordrin and the Clytin Claw Kobold Caper

## Spread 1: Meet the Adventurers

Eryneth the ranger, with a bow made of yew,
Aknemeia the sorcerer, whose magic was true.
Szeth the quick monk, who could leap through the air,
And Caryndal the cleric, with a kind, gentle stare.

The Keepers of Mordrin, as brave as can be,
Were walking along by a sunny green tree,
When they wandered right into a small halfling town,
Where the people were starting to point at the ground!

## Spread 2: The Sinking Town

A tavern was tipping! A bakery slanted!
The veggies were sinking where farmers had planted!
The mayor cried out, "What a terrible plight!
Our whole little town is just sinking from sight!"

The heroes looked down at the wobbling street,
And felt a great rumble right under their feet.
"The trouble," said Szeth, with a tap of his toe,
"Is coming from somewhere quite far down below!"

## Spread 3: Into the Depths

They found a dark cave just outside of the square,
And tiptoed inside with the greatest of care.
The tunnels were twisty, the shadows were long,
But the brave little Keepers knew they must be strong.

With Aknemeia's magic to light up the way,
They crept through the dirt and the dark and the clay.
"We must find the cause of the town's sinking woe,"
Said Caryndal softly, "But which way do we go?"

## Spread 4: The Kobold Encounter

Suddenly—*SNAP!* went a flimsy twig trap,
And a small orange creature peered out from a gap.
Mottled with grey scales and trembling with fear,
He shouted, "Go 'way! You are not welcome here!"

Then out popped his friends, a whole scaly crew,
Some orange, some striped, and a green one or two.
"I am brave Koldakask!" the first creature cried,
though he and his friends clearly wanted to hide.

## Spread 5: A Plea for Help

The heroes smiled kindly and sat on the floor,
And soon the cute kobolds were fearful no more.
"The Clytin Claw tribe," said young Koldakask, weeping,
"Has terrible trouble that keeps us from sleeping!"

He grabbed a big rock and began to draw near,
A picture of someone the kobolds all fear.
"It's Casarioth! The big dragon of black!
He eats all our food and he won't give it back!
He makes us dig tunnels that crumble and fall,
If you don't chase him off, he will swallow us all!"

## Spread 6: Confronting Casarioth

The Keepers stood up and they marched down the hall,
To face the mean dragon who frightened them all.
There in the shadows, with grumpy red eyes,
Lay Casarioth, hoarding a mountain of pies!

"You're eating too much, and you're digging too deep!"
Said Eryneth boldly, awaking his sleep.
The black dragon snorted a puff of dark smoke,
"I'll eat who I want!" the big grumpy beast spoke.

## Spread 7: Driving off the Dragon

But the clever young Keepers had tricks up their sleeve!
Bravery and feats the beast couldn't believe.
Aknemeia used magic to toast his mean toes,
While Szeth did a flip that went right past his nose!

Eryneth aimed her great bow up high,
And knocked a stalactite right into his pie!

Caryndal yelled with a booming, bright sound,
And the terrible dragon took off from the ground.
He flew from the cave with a whine and a huff,
Deciding the Keepers were simply too tough!

## Spread 8: Happily Ever After... with Rabbits

The town stopped its sinking, the houses stood straight,
And the Clytin Claw kobolds thought heroes were great.
Koldakask smiled beside his friend Meldriss so fair,
While petting a bunny with soft, fluffy hair.

"We promise to never dig under your street,
We'll just raise these bunnies," he chuckled, "so sweet!"
The heroes waved goodbye to the cavern of stone,
Leaving the kobolds to farm on their own.
