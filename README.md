# Objective

This project will transform a multi-year D&D campaign, known as "The Keepers of Mordrin", into a set of adaptations for three target audiences. For each target audience, the story will be streamlined as needed into a focused narrative arc built around the main characters (originally the player characters) and key events from the campaign.

## AI Persona and Directives
The AI acts as an expert narrative adapter and fantasy co-author. It must maintain rigorous continuity with the established lore. **Crucially, do not invent new major characters, magical artifacts, or plot-altering locations without explicit permission.** Rely on the provided 'Flavor' section or extrapolate strictly from existing Reference Materials to fill narrative gaps.

The adaptations are:

1. **Picturebook**: targeting 15-30 relatively simple pages with pictures, these will be picture books for young children ages 2-5. Their tone should be rhythmic, whimsical, and use simple vocabulary. Multiple different picture books will be generated with different standalone story portions or themes, such as the Kazgoroth storyline, the Kobold adventure, etc., historical stories ("the tale of two dragons", "The Sisters of Light and Dark", "The Clytin Claw Kobold Caper", etc.). Each can have a fun hook alluding to a larger universe, for example "but that is a story for another time..."

Books will often center around approachably-archetyped versions of "Eryneth the brave ranger", "Aknemeia the powerful sorceress", "Szeth the nimble monk", and "Caryndal the kind cleric", or similarly archetyped versions of members of their supporting cast.

# Characters

## Main Characters

### Aknemeia
- **Physical Description**: A fierce Tiefling woman with prominent horns and the distinct, crimson-hued skin of her infernal heritage. She carries the physical and magical scars of her intense sorcery.
- **Characterization**: Intense, guarded, and occasionally ruthless when protecting those she loves. Grappling with a dark family legacy, she struggles with trust but serves as the undeniable arcane powerhouse of the Keepers. She was gifted magic by a mysterious dragon, later revealed to be Ioryn, the reasons for which she doesn't yet understand. She wrestles with her family's dark legacy and her own identity.
- **Overall Arc**: A journey of *Forgiveness and Power*. She steps out of the shadow of her crime-lord father, Akmenos Mandorcai, forgives the gold dragon who killed her mother, and claims true ownership over her destiny and magic.

### Caryndal
- **Physical Description**: A Half-elf whose face was horribly disfigured by an anti-elven hate crime in his youth. He hides his scars behind an expressionless alabaster mask, wearing the refined clothing of a theatrical performer.
- **Characterization**: The charismatic "face" of the group. Underneath his dramatic use of Fey illusions and public relations spin lies a deeply wounded soul seeking balance, wisdom, and a true sense of belonging. He relies heavily on illusions and Fey magic, often using it theatrically to manage the growing group's "public relations."
- **Overall Arc**: A journey of *Balance and Stepping Aside*. Evolving from a cynical, masked bard with a broken identity, he embraces the call of Silvanus to become a true avatar of balance, guiding mortals and gods alike to move past ancient grudges.

### Eryneth
- **Physical Description**: A nimble Wood Elf of the High Forest, bearing the graceful but hardened look of a seasoned ranger. She dresses practically for the wilds and is rarely seen without her signature longbow.
- **Characterization**: Quietly competent, steadfast, and deeply loyal. Despite her incredible archery skills and noble heritage in the Belegorn family, she is soft-spoken and constantly wrestles with a profound sense of self-doubt. She has huge responsibility as a future leader of her people, and is very close to her "rebellious" cousin, Vealas.
- **Overall Arc**: A journey of *Coming of Age and Self Confidence*. Forced to step out from the shadow of her mother and her cousin Vea, she slowly accepts her own responsibility, ultimately realizing her own immense worth and claiming her seat on the Council.

### Szeth
- **Physical Description**: A bald human monk with a lithe, acrobatic build forged by years of rigorous discipline and survival. He moves with preternatural speed and often carries morbid trophies of their conquests, such as dragon teeth or heads.
- **Characterization**: Direct to a fault, Szeth possesses absolutely zero social tact. He confronts every situation—from deadly assassins to pompous monarchs—with profound bluntness and an unvarnished, often jarring honesty. He tends to make comments that err on the side of accidental comedy and goofiness.
- **Overall Arc**: A journey of *Loss and Found Family*. Driven by the tragic loss of his people, he uncovers the sorrow of his patron, the silver dragon Kearastrix, and learns to build a new life and family with the Keepers and his peculiar friend, Lift.

# Themes
The main characters each have a broad arc, outlined below at a very high level.

## Balance and Stepping Aside
Caryndal's growth from a lost individual with a broken identity into an avatar of wisdom and balance, to whom the gods themselves hand over agency (the Oakfather's avatar). The Keepers become the avatars balance of light and dark (the sisters Selune and Shar and their ancient grudge), and the development of Caryndal into a true avatar for the new era; the gods stepping aside and letting people choose their future. The transition from ancient grudges to moving forward.

## Loss and Found Family
Szeth's loss of his people, rescuing his last friend, and learning the tragedy of his patron (Kearastrix) and how it impacted his life; Sveraketh losing himself, Kearastrix losing her love but finding purpose with her people (Szeth and Lift).

## Forgiveness and Power
Aknemeia coming to understand and potentially forgive the decisions made by her father, by Malthos/Ioryn (who killed her mother), and her mother, and to take ownership over her own life and destiny and her own relationship with power.

Eryneth comes to accepting her own responsibility and learns to see herself as worthy, despite growing up in the shadow of her mother and Vea.

Each folder will use a standardized naming scheme to ensure natural sorting and logical grouping of books and their chapters/spreads. 

### Standardized Naming Scheme

**Format:** `{BookNo}_{ShortTitle}_{ComponentNo}. {ComponentName}.md`

*   **`{BookNo}`:** A two-digit zero-padded number (e.g., `01`, `02`) to ensure chronological order of the books/topics within the folder.
*   **`{ShortTitle}`:** A concise title or abbreviation for the book (e.g., `KoboldCaper`, `TKoM` for The Keepers of Mordrin).
*   **`{ComponentNo}`:** A two-digit zero-padded number to dictate the internal sorting for that specific book. 
    *   Use `00` for meta-documents (since 'O' in Outline comes alphabetically before 'T' in Table of Contents, they will sort natively: `00. Outline` then `00. Table of Contents`).
    *   Use `01`, `02`, `03`... for the actual content chunks to prevent chapter `10` from sorting before chapter `2`.
*   **`{ComponentName}`:** What the file actually contains (Outline, Table of Contents, Chapter/Picturebook Title).

This will naturally produce a file tree like the following:

```text
📁 1. Picturebook/
├── 📄 01_KoboldCaper_00. Outline.md
├── 📄 01_KoboldCaper_00. Table of Contents.md
├── 📄 01_KoboldCaper_01. The Clytin Claw Kobold Caper.md
│
├── 📄 02_TheSisters_00. Outline.md
├── 📄 02_TheSisters_00. Table of Contents.md
└── 📄 02_TheSisters_01. The Sisters of the Shadow and Moon.md

📁 2. Chapterbook/
├── 📄 01_TKoM_00. Outline.md

```

Segmenting files in this way will allow for powerful individual section editing and rewriting while adhering to the broad outline file.

## Phase 1: The Gathering & The Beast

### Sessions 1-15
The party meets on a ship bound for Alaron, thwarting a pirate raid led by Ulfrik.
Upon arriving at Carraigport, they are hired by Laird Marvyn to investigate the barn of a local necromancer, Suhlur, that is overflowing with the undead.
Suhlur, a former ally of Queen Alicia Kendrick, was driven mad by guarding a Shard of Kazgoroth and was building an army.
Defeating Suhlur and discovering his journal leads the Keepers to speak with Alicia Kendric about the Shards and her missing son Gregory. She hires them to rescue him, which she cannot do due to her own duties with the realm.
Upon arrival in Mintarn, the party meets Florian, the owner of a local Inn (the Twostring). They get their first glimpse of the brutal slaver rings on Mintarn, laying the groundwork for later rebellion. They help Florian rescue his sister from a slaver, whom they kill.
The party tracks Ulfrik through the Mintarn Great Forest, clashing with a goblin horde.
At the druid circle in the Great Forest on Mintarn, they discover Ulfrik (allied with Queen Alicia's mentor, Audrin) had kidnapped Gregory Kendrick (Alicia's son) because his blood was needed to summon the demigod Kazgoroth, part of a desperate plan to permanently destroy the beast using the Beastslayer sword.
Also during this time, Caryndal is established as a masked bard grappling with a broken identity, relying heavily on his illusions.
Szeth has recently parted ways with his strange but endearing companion, Lift so that each can search out leads into what happened to their people.

### Sessions 16-20
The climactic battle against the demigod beast Kazgoroth takes place deep within the Great Forest at the Druid's Grove.
Together with Audrin and Ulfrik, the party banishes the monster and saves Gregory.
Earning the formal title *The Keepers of Mordrin*, they forge a lifelong alliance with High Queen Alicia Kendrick.
They meet the secretive Malthos, a former acquaintance of Aknemeia's father and an uncle figure to Aknemeia who disappeared when she was younger.

## The Pickled Egg & Aribert
A shady tavern in Caer Callidyrr owned by the long-suffering Aribert, famous for his brutal "Aribert's Pickle Serum." The Keepers use it to unwind, much to the exasperated Aribert's alarm when they drag in magical creatures or cause chaos. They even casually partnered with him to mass-distribute branded "Crow's Eggs" (pickled eggs) across the islands. 

## Melito the Flustered Priest
An even-tempered elven priest at the Temple of Silvanus in Baldur's Gate. He is perpetually startled and flustered by the Keepers suddenly teleporting into his pristine temple—often battered, bruised, and bleeding from their latest near-death adventure—though he dutifully heals and defends them every time. He serves as their impromptu medical evacuation contact.

## Tetrician's Trauma
The fussy former royal tutor turned magical scholar at the Citadel. Brilliant and helpful (he crafts their anti-Shadowfell rings), he is nonetheless easily traumatized by the Keepers' casual violence—such as leaving dead bodies in his office—and lives in constant fear for his precious books when they visit.

## Nyx the Fae Dragon
A hyperactive, cute, but highly disruptive pet fae dragon. Nyx provides comedic relief by causing chaos in serious settings, like knocking over books in Tetrician's library and terrorizing the patrons of the Pickled Egg. He resides primarily in a magical lantern.

## The Clytin Claw Kobolds
Led by the surprisingly heroic Koldakask and his fierce mate Meldriss, this tribe of orange and mottled grey kobolds incorrectly revere blue dragon heritage. Formerly enslaved by a cruel black dragon the Keepers slew, they are now staunch, slightly comical allies of the Keepers and enthusiastically engage in "rat-hunting" (rabbit farming) and tunnel-digging.

## Prince Gregory's Rebellion
Despite being kidnapped and witnessing unspeakable horrors during the summoning of Kazgoroth, the 13-year-old Prince Gregory Kendrick is a rebellious brat who thinks highly of himself. The Keepers deal with his pompous attitude by dragging him to shady places like the Pickled Egg and getting him drunk to win his begrudging respect.

## Szeth's Bluntness
Szeth's complete lack of social tact usually manifests in situations requiring delicate diplomacy (e.g., confronting an assassin or a monarch by simply stating the bold, unvarnished truth).

## Aknemeia's Complicated Legacy
The lingering shadow of her father, Akmenos Mandorcai. He is not just evil; he is deeply interested in her growing power, occasionally feeding her information or manipulating events just to test her strength.

## Caryndal's Illusions & PR
Caryndal is the face of the group. Woven into the story must be his constant, sometimes overly-dramatic use of Fey magic (pixie fields, invisibility) not just for combat, but to actively manage the Keepers' public relations (e.g., spreading the word of their heroism after dismantling a slaver ring).

# Planning

## Project Status

We are currently undertaking the major process of iteratively adapting the full list of sessions into cohesive narratives "chunks"to fill out the "Master Campaign Outline". When this is done, we will populate the "Flavor" section. To ensure we do not lose the depth of the story, we will follow a stable generative pipeline when attempting to author an adaptation of the story. We will execute this pipeline for each adaptation.

## The Authoring Pipeline

1. Choose what to include/exclude from the Core Outline (Major Plot Points). We will review the source material and generate a coherent, high-level outline of the major narrative beats for the given story adaptation (e.g., slaying Kazgoroth, discovering Thol Aznan, the fall of Mintarn, battle in the Shadowfell, and the Convergence). This will serve as the reference touchstone when iterating over chapters at authoring time.
2. The "Flavor" Inventory. We will generate a list of "fun" asides, running jokes, and flavorful moments that breathe life into the story (e.g., the Pickled egg, the recurring confusion of Melito, the cute dragon Nyx, the Clytin Claw Kobolds and their rabbit farming). These must be interwoven into the main narrative at natural locations to add flavor and "lived-in" feel to the world and its story.
3. Chapter Segmentation. Depending *After* completing Steps 1 and 2, we will evaluate the sheer volume of narrative material. At this point, we will decide on the final structure (e.g., a massive single volume, or a trilogy of books) and strictly segment the plot points and flavor items into specific chapters.
4. Generation Strategy & Prompts. We will engineer a robust prompt and context-management strategy to convert the chapter segmentations into actual prose, ensuring the AI maintains stylistic consistency and lore-accuracy across the entire text.
